Kitchen Renovation

Full kitchen redesign — layout reconfiguration through to final detailing

A full kitchen redesign that began as a brief to refresh finishes and developed into a complete reconfiguration of the layout. Working through the existing space revealed an opportunity to improve flow, storage, and ergonomics by rethinking the floor plan rather than working within the original footprint.

Before

The existing kitchen and meals area prior to renovation. The first two images were taken at the time of purchase with the original styling in place; the remaining two show the space once vacated — with the meals furniture removed, the spatial constraints of the original layout become more apparent.

Existing conditions

  • Bold red ceramic tile splashback and grey stone-look laminate benchtop with a dated bullnose edge profile — a colour scheme that significantly ages the space
  • No designated pantry; storage limited to standard overhead and below-bench cabinetry with poor capacity
  • Restricted bench run with minimal usable preparation space
  • Dated 1¼ bowl stainless steel sink with drainer, top-mounted to the laminate benchtop — a functional and aesthetic liability
  • Oven positioned under the benchtop rather than at a comfortable working height
  • Meals area confined to a small corner — the footprint accommodated only a compact round table for four

Layout Reconfiguration

As part of the design development I proposed adjusting the floor plan to make better use of the available space.

Original layout
Proposed reconfiguration

I suggested this reconfiguration for several reasons:

  • Made use of the empty space separating the kitchen from the living area, which was previously an ineffective use of floor space
  • Relocated the meals area into that space, freeing up the original meals area to become additional kitchen floor space
  • Created the opportunity for a proper pantry, which the original layout did not allow for
  • Added bench space and reorganised the appliance layout so the oven is no longer positioned beneath the cooktop
  • Gave the microwave its own dedicated position, rather than sitting on the benchtop
  • Allowed drawers to replace cabinets wherever applicable in the lower cabinetry, improving everyday accessibility

Corner Storage

The lower corner cabinet presented a common problem — the geometry of an internal corner makes drawers impractical, leaving a standard hinged door as the only option and the space largely inaccessible. A Lehmann's corner unit was specified here: a swing-out carousel system that brings the full depth of the cabinet out to the user on open, eliminating the need to reach into a dark corner. The result is a practical appliance cupboard — slow cooker, rice cooker, and blender all stored at bench height and fully accessible without bending or rummaging.

Key outcomes

  • Reassessed the original brief and identified a full layout reconfiguration as the stronger design solution
  • Proposed relocating key appliances to improve ergonomics and workflow through the space
  • Developed the design from early inspiration and sketches through to resolved floor plan and joinery drawings
  • Selected and coordinated a cohesive material and finish palette across benchtop, cabinetry, and fittings
  • Carried the design through to completion, from concept to finished kitchen
