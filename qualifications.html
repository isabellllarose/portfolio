Beach House

225–227 Ocean Beach Road, Sorrento, VIC · Freehand drawing set · A01–A05

A01 — Site plan A02 — Floor plan A03–A04 — Elevations
A05 — Section

A freehand residential design project for a contemporary coastal holiday house at 225–227 Ocean Beach Road, Sorrento. The proposal responds to a client brief focused on accessible, adaptable living — balancing passive solar design, site response, and a relaxed coastal character.

Working freehand required a different kind of thinking to CAD — decisions about proportion and arrangement had to be resolved directly on the page. The section in particular required committing to ceiling heights, roof pitch, and floor levels without the ability to iterate digitally first.

Key outcomes

  • Produced a complete freehand design drawing set: site plan, floor plan, four elevations, and section
  • Applied passive solar design principles — north-facing living, shading, orientation to coastal breezes
  • Addressed accessible design requirements including 1:14 ramp grade and accessible bathroom
  • Demonstrated site-responsive thinking for a corner lot with dual street frontage

Richmond Townhouse — Town Planning

318 Burnley Street, Richmond VIC 3121 · AT 2.1 VU23452 · TP-00 to TP-07

Design Journal — 15 pages

Planning Drawing Set

TP-00 — Front page & neighbourhood character TP-01 — Site analysis plan TP-05 — Elevations (all four) TP-06 & TP-07 — Shadow diagrams

Residential town planning documentation for a contemporary townhouse development at 318 Burnley Street, Richmond — a narrow 305m² site in General Residential Zone 4. The project explored site analysis, neighbourhood character, overshadowing, and contextual response for a planning permit submission.

Burnley Street is a mixed-character street with both residential and commercial uses — bungalows, warehouses, medical centres, and places of worship. That inconsistency in neighbourhood character actually created more design freedom than a more uniform street would have allowed.

Key outcomes

  • Completed a detailed site survey and analysis plan for a narrow inner-suburban Melbourne lot
  • Produced shadow diagrams for 9AM and 3PM on 22nd September equinox
  • Assessed neighbourhood character, zoning constraints, and design opportunities
  • Prepared a town planning documentation set suitable for a permit application
  • Documented design development process through a 15-page design journal

Richmond Townhouse — Construction Documentation

318 Burnley Street, Richmond VIC 3121 · AT 2.2 · A01–A11

A01 — Site plan A02 — Ground floor plan A03 — First floor plan A05 — Elevations (North & East) A06 — Sections (Cross A-A, Cross C-C, Longitudinal B-B) A07 & A08 — Construction details
A09 — Ground floor electrical plan A10 & A11 — Schedules

Further refinement of the approved Richmond townhouse concept into a full construction documentation set. The project took the design from planning approval through to a coordinated working drawing set suitable for a building permit application — including structural coordination, services integration, and detailed specifications for materials, windows, and doors.

The two construction details — a first floor junction and a skylight — required resolving how assemblies meet at critical junctions, specifying insulation, sarking, flashing, and lining in sequence. The electrical plan and schedules demonstrate coordination across trades that a documentation set needs to achieve.

Key outcomes

  • Produced a coordinated 10-sheet documentation set from planning approval to building permit standard
  • Detailed two construction details at 1:10 — first floor junction (D01) and skylight (D02)
  • Produced ground floor electrical plan with full symbols legend and lighting fixture schedule
  • Completed window and door schedules specifying Stegbar, Rylock, Strata, and B&D products
  • Coordinated cladding, roofing, glazing, and structural specifications across the drawing set

Bushfire-Responsive Residential Design

31 Livingstone Close, Burwood VIC 3125 · AT 2.3 VU23459 · A01–A09

A01 — Drawing register & benefits statement A02 — Site plan to BAL 40 A08 & A09 — Construction details

Site planning and construction detailing for a dwelling at 31 Livingstone Close, Burwood, developed in accordance with AS 3959 — addressing both BAL 19 (lightweight) and BAL 40 (heavyweight) construction requirements to improve bushfire resilience and occupant safety.

The BAL 40 detail required a fundamentally different approach to materials: brick veneer walls with Hebel Powerfence boundary fencing, reinforced concrete driveway suitable for CFA truck load, dedicated fire water tanks with 65mm CFA hose coupling, and Eurotec roller shutters on all openings.

Key outcomes

  • Conducted a BAL assessment and prepared a benefits statement and drawing register
  • Produced a site plan incorporating CFA truck parking, 26,000L fire water tank, and defendable space
  • Detailed BAL 19 lightweight construction — FC sheet, embermesh, sarking, sealed junctions
  • Detailed BAL 40 heavyweight construction — brick veneer, roller shutters, slab-on-ground, non-combustible render
  • Applied AS 3959:2018 requirements systematically across site planning and building envelope

A commercial wellness centre concept exploring spatial zoning, materiality, and user experience through a calm and grounded architectural language. The building organises ritual-based zones — movement, heat, water, and rest — through a sequence of textured, tactile spaces animated by light, greenery, and curved form.

The project combined BIM modelling in ArchiCAD and presentation layout in Affinity to produce a cohesive submission. The A1 poster was designed as a standalone document — communicating design intent, atmosphere, floor plans, section, and all four elevations in a single composition. Visualisations were produced using ArchiCAD Cineware and AI-assisted rendering tools.

Commercial Construction Details

Technical documentation · 2026 · images/commercial-details/

Parapet detail — 1:10 Canopy detail — 1:10 Entry footing detail — 1:10

Three construction details for a commercial building, produced to working-drawing standard at 1:10 scale as part of the 22627VIC course at Holmesglen. The details address three distinct junctions — the parapet, the canopy, and the entry footing — each requiring resolution of how materials, structure, waterproofing, and finish layers meet at a critical point in the building envelope.

Key outcomes

  • Produced three 1:10 construction details resolving parapet, canopy, and entry footing junctions for a commercial building
  • Demonstrated understanding of how structural, waterproofing, and finish layers are sequenced and documented at a detail scale
  • Applied relevant NCC and BCA commercial building requirements to each detail
  • Coordinated material specifications and tolerances within the detail drawings
